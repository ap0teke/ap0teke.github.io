alparslan/apo/ap0-teke's fields of interest and practice extend beyond their formal education background in engineering and social sciences. Having an unquenchable thirst for learning, they


ap0teke performs solo and collaborative noise acts.
their primary instruments are a laptop and a no-input mixing console, most of the time accompanied by junk material and heavy electronics. believing in the progressive potentials of joining practice and theory, they aim to spark and engage in theoretical debates around the aesthetic-political implications of noise, music, collectivity, and technology. the ground on which such endeavors are made possible is to a large extent the A.I.D initiative, where ap0’ done his apprenticeship.




"Am I playing the machines? Or, are these machines playing me? [...] It seems like there is quite an equal relationship between these machines and me. And also I find the same degree of equality between my music and me. I don't actually care who is which and which is who."
Toshimaru Nakamura inResonance: Feedback

in my relationship with performance, and no-input mixing in particular. of choosing and performing instruments with an inherently unvirtuosable . Performing it, or performing with it? When it comes to no-input mixing board, especially in its bare, raw form, I reckon the former is the case. inconsumable and unpredictable outcomes. Complementing each other, these two provide a The former points to the feeling of coalescence which emerges at the most intense moments of playing. latter points to the relevance of the element of surprise and nonlinearity, which are inherent to positive feedback loops as in no-input mixing. still more fundamentally both of those views can be read as further problematizations of dichotomies of subject/object and necessary/contingent. no-input mixer is unmasterable; it defies virtuosity and by doing so offers an experience that is a continual steering at the limit. in this sense, it is an act of BwO;
"You never reach the Body without Organs, you can't reach it, you are forever attaining it, it is a limit." (1000yayla.p.150)
Playing is like going for walk each time on unknown terrain;
"While taking a stroll outdoors, on the other hand, he is in the mountains, amid falling snowfiakes, with other gods or without any gods at all, without a family, without a father or a mother, with nature. [...] Everything is a machine.[...] --all of them connected to those of his body. The continual whirr of machines. [...] There is no such thing as either man or nature now, only a process that produces the one within the other and couples the machines together. Producing-machines, desiring-machines everywhere, schizophrenic machines, all of species life: the self and the non-self, outside and inside, no longer have any meaning whatsoever (antiodip.p.9)

i have done nothing but touching to points that weren't meant to touch forever. THIS IS WHAT YOUR KEYBOARDS HIDE FROM YOU. THE MACHINE FINALLY COMES ONTO ITS OWN. all played by two chips and a bunch of capacitors and resistors laying beneath black and white keys of a Casio SA-1 keyboard.
